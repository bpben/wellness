A variety of services are important in order to provide employees at all levels of risk and with a number of conditions to engage with the program.[1] Research suggests that programs offering a broad array of programs had higher participation[2] and are more likely to have an effect on employee health behavior.[3] Surveys of employees have found that they prefer programs with a variety of services and ones that allow them to involve their families (e.g. health fairs).[4][5] Some examples of services include education materials, healthy options in snack machines and canteens and on-site health clinics.
