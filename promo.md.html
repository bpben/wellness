Promotion efforts (e.g. health fairs. newsletters, e-mails) are important parts of a comprehensive wellness program. There is ample evidence from the health promotion literature of the effectiveness of environmental changes such as informational posters and educational materials.[1][2][3] Additionally, employees list having opportunities to participate in a program during worktime as one of the most desirable elements of a wellness program.[4] Accordingly, many of the best practices guidance recommends extensive communication strategies and multiple opportunities to engage in a program.[5][6]
